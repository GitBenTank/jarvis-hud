## What investors are likely thinking (by beat)

**First ~10 seconds — capability**

They hear: agents taking real actions (email, systems, workflows).

- Passes “is this relevant?” — real, not purely theoretical.
- Open question: “Is this just another agent tool?” (you answer with the wedge, not here.)

**Infrastructure / market validation**

They hear: enterprises building registries, catalogs, governance layers.

- Signals category awareness and alignment with how buyers are spending.
- Reduces “toy startup” risk.

**Wedge — “they don’t control what happens at the moment…” / “that’s the gap”**

- Reframe: different **layer**, not a replacement for cloud catalogs.
- Platform potential shows up here — **if delivery is calm and explicit.**

**Risk — “independently verified”**

- Legitimizes enterprise urgency without naming vendors.
- May still prompt: “how painful is this **today**?” (prepare 1–2 concrete buyer stories.)

**Jarvis — “governs execution”**

- Clear positioning.
- Likely probes: **enforceability**, **build vs buy**, **why not AWS adds a checkbox**, **defensibility / moat.**

**Demo**

- Clean demo: proves execution layer is real, not vaporware.
- Sloppy demo: “interesting idea, not ready.”

**Close — proof vs logs**

- Memorable differentiation.
- Lingering question: “can this be a **company**?” (traction, ICP, expansion.)

**Desired takeaway after a strong meeting**

> “This is a **missing control layer** in the agent stack” — not “cool feature,” “generic agent tool,” or “workflow automation.”

---

## Honest pushback to prepare for

1. **Too abstract** — counter with one concrete path (you have demo + receipts).
2. **Pain / urgency** — “Who is **buying** this **now**?” Have names, use cases, and why they cannot wait.
3. **Moat** — “Why can’t **hyperscaler** ship this?” Answer: workflow integration, trust model, depth of proof/trace, speed; not hand-waving.

## 10-slide deck skeleton (max)

| # | Slide | Core message |
|---|--------|----------------|
| 1 | **Title** | Jarvis — control plane for AI **execution** |
| 2 | **The shift** | Agents moving from generation → **action** (email, systems, workflows) |
| 3 | **The problem** | Execution not controlled: no explicit approval boundary; **logs ≠ proof** |
| 4 | **Market validation** | Enterprises investing in agent infrastructure — registries, orchestration, governance |
| 5 | **The gap** | Nothing controls execution **at the moment it happens** — **visibility ≠ control** |
| 6 | **Solution** | Jarvis = approval + proof — **propose → approve → execute → receipt → trace** |
| 7 | **Demo** | UI: approval → execute → receipt (screenshot or short clip) |
| 8 | **Why now** | Capability ↑, adoption ↑, governance pressure ↑ |
| 9 | **Why this wins** | Agent-agnostic; sits between intent and execution; **authority boundaries** |
| 10 | **Vision** | Production AI actions are **governed** — control + audit + trust layer |

**Optional visual — stack**

```text
Agents (models, frameworks, tools)
        ↓
Platforms (cloud catalogs, orchestration)
        ↓
Jarvis — execution control + proof
        ↓
Real systems (email, APIs, infra, production)
```
