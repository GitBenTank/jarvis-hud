# Pitch narrative outline (deck-friendly)

Use this as **speaker notes** and slide order. Keep slides visual; spoken narrative carries the Thesis Lock story. Full competitive context: [competitive-landscape-2026.md](./competitive-landscape-2026.md).

**Principle:** The demo is the proof. Slides set context; the live or recorded path (proposal → approval → execution → receipt → trace) is the anchor—do not bury it in an appendix.

**Accelerator / five-slide compression:** [gener8tor-pitch.md](./gener8tor-pitch.md) (consequence-before-UI, slide copy, timed demo blocks).

**Room discipline** (opener, 30s pitch, objections): [room-playbook-v1.md](./room-playbook-v1.md).

---

## Recommended flow (demo central)

1. **Problem** — Unauthorized or unaccountable execution as agents gain real permissions.
2. **Risk** — Intelligence scaled faster than authority and evidence; “governance” language is everywhere, proof is not.
3. **Jarvis concept** — Control plane, not agent framework; Thesis Lock in one breath (propose → approve → execute → receipt → trace; model is not the trust root).
4. **Live proof** — **The demo is the proof.** Proposal lands → pending → human approve → explicit execute → receipt → trace. Optional: blocked path without approval. *This is the heart of the pitch.*
5. **Market** — Three waves of competition; why incumbents optimize for breadth; Jarvis wedge (authority + proof, governs proposals not runtimes).
6. **Ask** — Fundraising, design partners, or OSS—one clear ask.

---

## 1. Title

- **Headline:** Approval and proof for AI-driven action.
- **Sub:** Jarvis HUD — control plane, not agent framework.
- **Say:** We are not competing to “build the best agent.” We govern **whether actions run** and **what proof exists after.**

---

## 2. Problem

- Agents gained **real permissions** (tools, files, APIs). The failure mode is no longer “bad text”—it is **unauthorized or unaccountable execution**.
- **Say:** Intelligence scaled faster than **authority and evidence.**

---

## 3. Risk / why now

- Incumbents validate governance vocabulary; category is real; the risk for a focused product is **sounding generic**, not “no demand.”
- **Say:** Window for a **proof-first** wedge is open; claims without artifacts lose to noise.

---

## 4. Jarvis concept (compress Thesis Lock + product)

- **Thesis Lock (six bullets)** on one slide if needed: propose anything; execution requires explicit approval; approval ≠ execution; receipts; model not trusted principal; autonomy in thinking, authority in action.
- **Product one-liner:** Ingress of proposals, human gate, policy, orchestrated execution, receipts, traces.
- **Say:** **Jarvis governs proposals, not runtimes**—same boundary across agent stacks.

---

## 5. Live proof (non-negotiable main event)

**The demo is the proof.** Prefer live or a tight recording—minimal slides here.

**Fixed script (one line each—run the same path every time):**

1. OpenClaw proposes (ingress lands a proposal in Jarvis).
2. Jarvis shows **pending** (nothing executed yet).
3. Operator **approves** (authority boundary / human gate).
4. **Execution** runs—or show **blocked** when policy denies (same invariant: no silent run).
5. **Receipt** and **trace** appear (Trace ID, outcome, artifact; note **rollback** path when applicable).

- **Say:** Never claim what you do not show. “Audit” here means **reconstructable outcomes**.

---

## 6. Market / competition (after proof)

- Three waves: enterprise suites, agent platforms, execution startups (see competitive memo).
- **Say:** Jarvis is narrow on purpose: **authority boundary + proof**, not another builder.

---

## 7. Differentiation (optional slide)

- Approval-first **system** vs approval **feature**.
- Receipts/traces vs logs only.
- Explicit **approve then execute** vs implicit runs.

---

## 8. Go-to-market / ICP (light)

- Platform teams, security-conscious builders, compliance-adjacent workflows.
- **Say:** Best adopters already fear **silent execution**.

---

## 9. Ask

- Tailor: fundraising, design partners, or OSS adoption—state the **one** ask clearly.

---

## Appendix (optional slides)

- OpenClaw (or other) as **capability layer**, Jarvis as **authority layer** (diagram from README).
- Operator legibility: origin mismatch, integration readiness—**machine truth**, mismatch-only UI.
